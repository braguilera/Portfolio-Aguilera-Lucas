Feature Article

Celebrating Philadelphia's History with Art

Lucas Aguilera, an artist from Washington Township, NJ, painted a 26-foot mural inside Tioga-Franklin Savings Bank in Philadelphia's Fishtown neighborhood. Celebrating the bank's 150th anniversary, the mural features Philly icons like Rocky and the 1974 Stanley Cup-winning Flyers, as well as personal and historical references. This intricate artwork, Aguilera's first major project, reflects his learning journey and dedication, earning him recognition and acclaim in the artistic community.

Frequent questions

Whether you're interested in commissioning a custom piece or curious about my artistic process, here are answers to some of the most common questions I receive. If you have any other inquiries, feel free to reach out through the contact information provided.

What is the process for commissioning a custom artwork?

To commission a custom artwork, simply reach out to me through the contact form or email provided on this page. We'll discuss your vision, preferences, and any specific details you want included. I'll provide you with a quote and timeline. Once we agree on the terms, I'll begin working on your piece, keeping you updated throughout the process.

Can you create artwork from a photo?

Yes, I can create custom artwork from photos. Simply provide me with a high-quality image, and I'll use it as a reference to capture the essence and details in the final piece. Whether it's a portrait, mural, or another type of artwork, I'll ensure it reflects the subject accurately.

What payment methods do you accept?

I accept various payment methods, including bank transfers, PayPal, and major credit cards. For larger projects, a deposit may be required upfront, with the balance due upon completion. We will discuss and agree on the payment terms before starting the project to ensure a smooth transaction.
